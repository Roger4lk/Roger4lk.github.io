Predictive Stock-Market Model Details

What This Project is:

I developed a sophisticated predictive stock market model that leverages both macroeconomic data and LinkedIn job posting analytics to forecast 30-day forward returns. This project represents a unique intersection of financial analysis, machine learning, and labor market intelligence.

The model combines traditional financial indicators with innovative labor engagement signals to create a comprehensive prediction system. By analyzing job posting views, application rates, and macroeconomic trends, the model identifies patterns that traditional financial models often miss.

Technical Implementation:

The core of the system is a 100-tree Random Forest model built using scikit-learn, which processes data from multiple sources including yfinance for market data and LinkedIn's job posting analytics. The model achieved impressive performance metrics with R² ≈ 0.37 and MAE ≈ 6.6% on 30-day forward returns, significantly exceeding the hedge-fund relevance threshold of 0.30.

Key Findings & Impact:

The most significant discovery was the dominance of labor-engagement signals as predictors. Job-posting views (44.9% importance) and application rates (41.8%) together explained 87% of the model's predictive lift. This insight demonstrates how labor market dynamics can serve as leading indicators for market performance.

Performance & Validation:

The model significantly outperformed traditional baselines, boosting out-of-sample R² from 0.007 to 0.362 compared to a macro-only model. It also nearly eliminated naive/ARIMA errors, demonstrating the value of incorporating labor market intelligence into financial forecasting.

What I Learned:

This project deepened my understanding of machine learning in financial applications, feature engineering, and the importance of domain expertise in model development. I gained valuable experience with ensemble methods, hyperparameter tuning, and the challenges of working with financial time series data. The project also taught me about the critical importance of model interpretability in financial applications and how to communicate complex technical results to stakeholders.
